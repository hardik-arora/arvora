      🔄 GeoNames Dataset Pipeline
      
        
      
          How the 15,000 city database was prepared from the raw GeoNames database (IN.zip):
        
      
        
        
          
      • Downloading & Extraction: Fetched IN.zip containing IN.txt, representing Indian geographical features.
        • 
          
      • Populated Place Filtering: Filtered class 'P' records (populated places/cities), discarding administrative entities, lakes, and monuments.
        • 
          
      • Heuristic Sorting: Prioritized capitals (State and Country levels) first, then sorted in descending order of census population.
        • 
          
      • Cleaning & Deduplication: Normalized strings to lowercase, collapsed spaces, stripped punctuation, and extracted the top 15,000 unique names.
        •

      🔍 Authenticity Verification Guide
      
          
          
      
            
      
              🧣 Pashmina Shawls
              
      A real Pashmina thread is extremely fine. Test: pass it through a finger ring, or check for irregular weaves which indicate handloom work rather than machines.
      
            
      

            
      
              🌱 Saffron (Kesar)
              
      Real saffron threads do not dissolve instantly in warm water/milk, but slowly discharge yellow color while remaining red. Fake saffron dissolves and fades immediately.
      
            
      

            
      
              💎 Silk Textiles
              
      Burn test: burn a single thread. Pure silk smells like burnt hair and leaves crumbly black ash. Synthetic silk smells like plastic and melts into hard beads.

      ⚠️ Decline DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion)
      
          
      
            When withdrawing cash, the ATM may ask: "Do you want to be billed in your home currency?".
            

      
            Always select DECLINE CONVERSION / BILL IN LOCAL CURRENCY (INR).

      🏦 Forex ATM & DCC Advisor
      
        
      Avoid the hidden 5-10% dynamic markup fees at ATMs and payment terminals in India.
      
      
      

      
      
        
        
      
          
      💡 What is the DCC Trap?
      
          
      When using foreign cards at ATMs or shops in India, the machine offers to charge you in your home currency (USD/EUR) instead of Indian Rupees (INR). This is called Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC).
      
          
      
            🔴 THE TRAP: The merchant sets their own exchange rate containing a massive 5% to 10% markup hidden in the conversion rate!
          
      
          
      
            🟢 THE RULE: Always choose Local Currency (INR) and decline DCC conversions. Let your home bank handle the foreign exchange rate.
